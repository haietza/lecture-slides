Course Overview

  • The senior capstone project provides the student an independent and collaborative software development experience with a significant project.
  • The course introduces aspects of project management, requirements analysis, and the software lifecycle, but will primarily be concerned with the practical integration of core theories, practices, and ethics of the discipline.
  • Writing and speaking communication skills are reinforced.

Course Overview

  • Your project will be developed over about 15 calendar weeks with each class meeting consisting of a variety of presentation types from brief informal meetings to formal presentations with accompanying slides and work time. Your project will require work time outside of class, so plan accordingly.

Project Overview

  • The senior capstone project provides the student an opportunity to develop a significant software project as part of a small team.
  • Students may work alone with prior approval, but are strongly encouraged to work in teams of two.

Grading & Deliverables

  • Grades will break down as follows:
    • 60% - Project Results and Deliverables
      • Final technical report, project demonstration, and oral defense
    • 20% - Project Presentations
      • Includes initial and final presentations
    • 10% - Project Proposal
      • Technical document giving context and background to the project
    • 10% - Progress Meetings
      • Assessed on project management and attendance
  • The difficulty and complexity of the proposed project will be taken into account in this assessment

Grading & Deliverables

  • You will be graded on the following criteria:
    • Quality and timeliness of carrying out development goals, demonstrated by delivering assignments on time, progress tracking, and software developed.
    • Initiative, in learning new skills, creatively solving problems, and overcoming challenges
    • Integrity, in crediting others for their work, contributing appropriately to the team effort, and honestly accounting for ones own work
    • Quality of communication, both with your instructor and with your team members, as well as presentations, technical demonstrations, and standup meetings
    • Final results of the project, including the technical (written) report of the project and the formal (oral) presentation

Grading & Deliverables

  • The general structure of the semester will be as follows:
    • Week 1: Introduction (you are here)
    • Week 2: Informal brainstorming meetings
    • Week 3: Project proposals due and initial presentations
    • Week 4: Beginning of weekly standup meetings
    • End of Semester: Technical report due, project deliverables due
    • End of Semester: Project demonstration and oral defense
    • Final Exam: Final project presentation
